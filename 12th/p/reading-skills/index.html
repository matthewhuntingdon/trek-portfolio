Citation
History.com Staff. (2011).Weapons of the Vietnam War. Retrieved March 05, 2015, from http://www.history.com/topics/Vietnam-war/weapons-of-the-Vietnam-war

Summary
“The Vietnam War was more devastating than those of any previous conflict”. The U.S., and South Vietnam relied greatly on their air forces. This led to the use of anti-aircraft missiles. Both sides used everything they could to their advantage. The U.S. used extremely toxic defoliants to reduce the cover the Vietnamese armies could use. The Vietnamese set up booby traps that used sharpened bamboo, or a tripwire and cross bow to injure/kill enemies.

Evaluation
The source is useful because it provides not only information about the weapons used in the Vietnam War, but how they were used, where, and why. This information is particularly useful for me, because I chose to write about “technology and war”. The source is nicely divided into sections. This helped me find the information i needed quickly. The Source also provided many useful facts such as “19 million gallons of herbicides over 4.5 million acres of land in Vietnam from 1961 to 1972”. It helped me get a better understanding of the war as a whole. This made me see just how devastating war truly is, and made me wonder why we got into this war in the first place.
