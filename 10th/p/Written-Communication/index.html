Written Communication | Ukraines refugee crisis

Intro to Ukraine’s refugee crisis
Due to conflict with Russian backed separatists an estimated 2.6 million Ukrainians have fled from the east border of the Ukraine to seek refuge. More than 1.4 million of those people are still in the country. These people are known as Internally Displaced People (IDP). This is because when people flee from the conflict on the east border they head to the west of the country hoping to seek refuge in the EU. The problem is that the Ukraine isn't part of the EU, so these refugees are left with two options; go back across the country and through the conflict to seek refuge with Russia, or live on the west side of Ukraine in horrible living conditions. The only sustainable way to address this issue is by ending the conflict through federalization.

Understanding the crisis
The origin of the conflict between Russia and the Ukraine is one that separates the whole country. The problem is that most of the population in the east wants to be closer aligned with Russia, while others wants to be closer aligned with the EU and the West. This has caused conflict on the eastern border, forcing refugees to flee to the west of the country.

Currently, the UNHCR is providing people with housing and non food items (NFI) such as beds, as well as financial assistance, and compensation for medical examinations. The problem is that the UNHCR is vastly under funded. They have only reached 56% of the USD 41.5 million goal.

While it is important for someone to be taking care of the refugees, it is not sustainable as long as the conflict continues. While the conflict continues, people will continue to be forced to leave their homes and the cost to support them will increase. Seeing as the UNHCR is already underfunded, with more refugees their quality of life will worsen. The only way to take care of this situation is by addressing the root causes.

Proposed solution
I propose that to keep the quality of life for Internally Displaced People from worsening, the Ukraine should implement a new method of government called Federalization. This is a way of decentralizing the government which would give specific geographic locations more control over how they are governed. Each area would have control over things such as planning regional development, culture, sport, and tourism.

Federalization could very quickly and easily be implemented in the region. The current government would separate itself from the territory on the east. This would give the east near-independence which would allow them to closer align with Russia.

Implementing federalization would instantly halt the conflict along the east border and the stream of Internally Displaced People with it. It would also reduce tension in the region, especially with Russia and Russian-backed Separatists located in Ukraine. Federalization would also mean that Ukraine could join the UN without Russia imposing trade sanctions.
With the conflict resolved, the UNHCR could then focus more on relocating Internally Displaced People into permanent housing.
